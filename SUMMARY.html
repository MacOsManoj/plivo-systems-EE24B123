Plivo Systems Assignment — The Flaky Network

Executive Summary

This submission implements a high-performance, low-latency C++17 sender/receiver pipeline over UDP designed to withstand hostile network conditions (drops, jitter, reordering, and duplication). By pairing continuous Stride-1 XOR Forward Error Correction (FEC) with an Adaptive Jitter Buffer-inspired NACK retransmission engine, the solution achieves zero-buffer immediate playout with minimal playout delay.

Key Architectural Innovations

  • Zero-Overhead Wire Format: Transmits 164-byte packets (4-byte Big-End sequence header + 160-byte payload). The Most Significant Bit (MSB) flags FEC parity frames, eliminating protocol header bloat.
  • 6/7 Rate Bandwidth Bounding: FEC parity frames are generated for 6 out of every 7 frames, strictly capping baseline bandwidth at 1.90x and preserving 0.10x bandwidth headroom for ARQ/NACK retransmissions.
  • AJB-Inspired Dynamic Transit Estimation: Receiver calculates an Exponential Moving Average (EMA) of real packet transit times to adaptively trigger NACKs based on network jitter rather than fixed static timers.
  • Immediate Playout & Cascade Recovery: Frames are pushed to the player port (47020) instantly upon receipt or XOR recovery. Recovered frames recursively trigger cascade checks for adjacent lost slots.
